The Homeowner's Problem

Sarah M. was told by three contractors that her home's original windows were unsalvageable. The 1890s Colonial on Broad Street had beautiful original windows, but years of neglect and water infiltration had taken their toll. The bottom rails of 15 windows were severely rotted, and several sashes were sticking so badly they couldn't be opened.

Sarah's Dilemma:

"I was heartbroken at the thought of replacing these gorgeous original windows, but everyone said there was no other option. The quotes for replacement were over $15,000, and I knew it would destroy the character of my historic home. I felt like I had no choice but to accept the inevitable loss of these irreplaceable architectural features."

When Sarah contacted us for a second opinion, she was prepared to make the difficult decision to replace her windows. However, our assessment revealed that surgical restoration was not only possible but would preserve 95% of the original material while solving all functional issues.

Our Assessment & Plan

Our team conducted a thorough inspection of all 24 windows in Sarah's home, documenting the specific issues with each unit. The assessment revealed:

Severe Bottom Rail Rot

Advanced soft rot in the bottom rails of 15 windows, compromising structural integrity but limited to specific areas.

Sticking Sashes

Swollen sashes and worn hardware making 8 windows nearly impossible to operate properly.

Our assessment identified that the rot was localized and treatable through surgical epoxy consolidation rather than complete replacement. We developed a comprehensive restoration plan that included:

  • Selective removal of deteriorated wood sections using precision techniques
  • Epoxy consolidation to strengthen remaining sound wood structure
  • Traditional mortise and tenon joinery for wood patch installation
  • Complete sash restoration including hardware replacement and finishing
  • Historic glazing with period-appropriate materials and techniques

This approach would preserve the historical integrity of the windows while ensuring decades of continued service life.

The Restoration Process

Our 6-week restoration project involved precision craftsmanship and specialized techniques to bring Sarah's windows back to their original beauty and functionality.

Epoxy Consolidation

Advanced conservation-grade epoxy applied to strengthen deteriorated wood while preserving original profiles.

Traditional Wood Patching

Quartersawn white oak patches installed using mortise and tenon joinery to match original construction.

Historic Glazing

Traditional linseed oil glazing compound applied with authentic tooling techniques for period appearance.

Each window was carefully removed and worked on individually in our climate-controlled workshop. The process included:

  1. Documentation and numbering of each sash for proper reinstallation
  2. Precision excavation of rotted wood sections using specialized tools
  3. Application of conservation-grade epoxy consolidation materials
  4. Installation of traditional wood patches with hand-fitted joinery
  5. Hardware restoration and replacement with period-appropriate components
  6. Surface preparation and application of historically compatible finishes

The entire process was documented with before, during, and after photography to ensure complete transparency and quality control.

The Transformation
