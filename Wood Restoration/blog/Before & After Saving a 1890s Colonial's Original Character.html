Unique Challenges We Addressed

Advanced Wood Rot

Multiple windows suffered from severe soft rot in the sills and lower sash rails, requiring selective replacement using traditional mortise and tenon joinery.

Historic Hardware Issues

Original hardware was corroded and missing parts. We sourced authentic reproductions and restored what could be salvaged.

Paint System Failure

Eight layers of incompatible paint had trapped moisture, causing wood movement and seal failure throughout the window system.

Weatherproofing Deficiencies

Lack of proper weatherstripping allowed air infiltration and water penetration, compromising both comfort and preservation.

Operational Problems

Swollen sashes and worn tracks made windows difficult or impossible to operate, defeating their functional purpose.

Historic Compliance

All work needed to meet local historic preservation guidelines while achieving modern performance standards.

Our Comprehensive Restoration Process

Detailed Assessment

We conducted a thorough inspection of all 24 windows, documenting specific issues and developing individual treatment plans for each unit while maintaining architectural consistency.

Gentle Paint Removal

Using chemical strippers and hand tools, we carefully removed layers of old paint without damaging the underlying wood or historic profiles.

Selective Wood Replacement

Rotted sections were precisely removed and replaced with quartersawn white oak using traditional mortise and tenon joinery to match original construction methods.

Structural Reinforcement

Epoxy consolidation and wood hardeners were applied to strengthen remaining sound wood and prepare surfaces for new components.

Hardware Restoration

Original hardware was removed, cleaned, and refinished where possible. Missing pieces were reproduced using period-appropriate materials and techniques.

Weatherproofing Installation

Custom-fitted weatherstripping was installed using materials that complement the historic character while providing excellent air and water sealing.

Finishing and Protection

Three coats of premium alkyd paint in historically appropriate colors were applied, with careful attention to detail and period techniques.
