Warning Signs That Require Professional Attention

Sticking or Jammed Windows

When your windows no longer open or close smoothly, it's often due to seasonal wood movement, paint buildup, or worn hardware rather than structural failure. Forcing operation can cause permanent damage to the window frame and sash.

Professional Solution:

Expert restoration includes careful adjustment of sash weights, replacement of worn hardware, and precise planing of swollen wood to restore smooth operation without compromising structural integrity.

Visible Drafts and Energy Loss

Feeling cold air around your windows doesn't necessarily mean they're beyond repair. Drafts are typically caused by missing or deteriorated weatherstripping, gaps in the sash fit, or failed paint seals that can be addressed through restoration.

Professional Solution:

Proper restoration includes installing historically appropriate weatherstripping, adjusting sash fit, and sealing gaps while maintaining the window's original operation and appearance for improved energy efficiency.

Peeling Paint and Finish Failure

Multiple layers of incompatible paint, trapped moisture, or poor surface preparation often lead to peeling, blistering, or alligatoring paint. This not only looks unsightly but also exposes wood to moisture damage.

Professional Solution:

Professional paint system restoration involves careful stripping, wood preparation, and application of compatible primer and finish coats using techniques that preserve historic profiles and details.

Soft or Crumbling Wood

Localized wood rot or soft spots in window frames and sills are often repairable through selective replacement of damaged sections. Complete window replacement is rarely necessary for isolated wood deterioration.

Professional Solution:

Expert restoration uses traditional joinery techniques to replace only damaged wood sections with matching materials, preserving original profiles and maintaining historical authenticity.

Broken Hardware or Missing Parts

Worn latches, broken locks, or missing hardware components don't mean your windows are obsolete. Many original hardware pieces can be restored, and missing parts can be authentically reproduced.

Professional Solution:

Specialized restoration includes hardware repair, reproduction of missing period-appropriate components, and installation of compatible modern upgrades that maintain historical appearance.
