Advanced Technology for Historic Preservation

Epoxy consolidation is a cutting-edge preservation technique that strengthens deteriorated wood while maintaining its original appearance and structural integrity. This specialized process is essential for treating wood rot, insect damage, and general deterioration in historic windows, doors, and architectural elements without compromising their historical significance.

Unlike traditional methods that require complete replacement of damaged sections, epoxy consolidation allows us to preserve the maximum amount of original material while creating a stable, long-lasting repair that can withstand the test of time.

Our Epoxy Consolidation Process

Comprehensive Assessment

We begin with a detailed inspection to identify areas of wood deterioration, assess the extent of damage, and determine the most appropriate consolidation approach. This includes moisture testing and structural evaluation.

Precise Excavation

Soft, deteriorated wood is carefully removed using specialized tools, leaving only sound material as the foundation for consolidation. This ensures proper epoxy penetration and adhesion.

Surface Preparation

The exposed wood is thoroughly cleaned and prepared to ensure optimal epoxy adhesion. This may include light sanding, removal of contaminants, and moisture control.

Epoxy Application

High-quality, conservation-grade epoxy is carefully applied using low-pressure injection techniques to penetrate deep into the wood structure, filling voids and strengthening fibers.

Curing & Monitoring

The epoxy is allowed to cure under controlled conditions, with careful monitoring to ensure proper penetration and structural development. Additional applications may be made as needed.

Surface Finishing

Once fully cured, the consolidated areas are shaped, sanded, and prepared for final finishing. The repair is virtually invisible and ready for paint or stain application.

The Science Behind Epoxy Consolidation

Our epoxy consolidation process uses advanced conservation-grade materials specifically formulated for historic preservation applications. These epoxies penetrate deep into deteriorated wood fibers, creating a strong bond that restores structural integrity while remaining reversible for future conservation work.

The low-viscosity formulations flow easily into voids and damaged areas, displacing moisture and air to create a solid, stable matrix. Once cured, the consolidated wood becomes stronger than the original material while maintaining its dimensional stability and workability for subsequent finishing operations.

This technique is widely recognized by historic preservation professionals and meets the standards of leading preservation organizations. It's particularly effective for treating advanced wood rot, insect damage, and general deterioration in historic architectural elements.
