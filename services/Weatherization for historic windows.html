Balancing Comfort and Character

Weatherization for historic windows requires a delicate balance between improving energy efficiency and preserving architectural authenticity. Unlike modern windows that rely on sealed units and rigid construction, historic windows were designed to work with natural materials and seasonal movement. Our weatherization services address air infiltration, thermal performance, and moisture management while maintaining the operational characteristics and visual appeal that make your windows historically significant.

Proper weatherization can reduce energy costs by up to 25% while eliminating drafts, condensation, and noise infiltration. Our approach ensures your historic windows perform like new while retaining their original charm and character.

Our Historic Window Weatherization Process

Comprehensive Energy Audit

We begin with a detailed assessment using thermal imaging and air leakage testing to identify specific areas of energy loss and draft infiltration in your historic windows.

Operational Analysis

Each window is evaluated for proper operation, sash fit, and hardware functionality. Issues affecting weatherization performance are identified and addressed.

Custom Weatherstripping Installation

Historically appropriate weatherstripping materials are carefully selected and installed to provide maximum air sealing while maintaining proper window operation.

Seal and Caulk Assessment

Exterior and interior sealing points are evaluated and renewed with compatible materials that allow for natural wood movement and expansion.

Storm Window Integration

Where appropriate, custom storm windows are designed and installed to provide additional thermal protection while maintaining historical appearance.

Final Performance Testing

Air leakage and thermal performance are retested to ensure optimal results, with adjustments made as needed for maximum effectiveness.
