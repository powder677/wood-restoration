Preserving the Heart of Historic Windows

Historic glazing is a specialized craft that requires deep understanding of period materials, techniques, and aesthetics. Unlike modern window installations, historic glazing involves working with original glass, traditional putties, and authentic profiles that define your home's architectural character. Proper glazing not only ensures weatherproofing and energy efficiency but also maintains the visual authenticity that makes historic homes irreplaceable.

Our expert glaziers combine traditional craftsmanship with modern knowledge to deliver results that exceed both historical standards and contemporary performance expectations. Our Historic Glazing Process

Historical Assessment

We begin by evaluating your windows' historical period, original glazing methods, and existing conditions. This includes identifying appropriate materials, profiles, and techniques that match your home's architectural style and era.

Glass Selection & Preparation

Original glass is preserved where possible, or historically appropriate replacements are sourced. Glass is carefully measured, cut, and prepared to fit traditional glazing rabbets and profiles.

Putty Preparation

Traditional linseed oil-based glazing compounds are prepared and worked to proper consistency. Modern alternatives are used only when historically appropriate and structurally necessary.

Careful Installation

Glass is installed using traditional techniques, including proper bedding, glazing points, and compound application. Attention is paid to sight lines, profiles, and period-appropriate details.

Tooling & Finishing

Glazing compound is precisely tooled using traditional methods to create authentic profiles and proper sealing. Final finishing ensures smooth, professional appearance.

Curing & Painting

Proper curing time is allowed for oil-based compounds, followed by appropriate priming and painting with historically compatible finishes that provide long-term protection.
